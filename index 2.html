KASHMIR ON WORLD MAP

OVERVIEW :

Kashmir is the northernmost geographical region of the Indian subcontinent. Until the mid-19th century, the term Kashmir denoted only the Kashmir Valley between the Great Himalayas and the Pir Panjal Range. The term has since also come to encompass a larger area that formerly comprised the princely state of Jammu and Kashmir, and includes the Indian-administered territories of Jammu and Kashmir and Ladakh, the Pakistani-administered territories of Azad Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan, and the Chinese-administered territories of Aksai Chin and the Trans-Karakoram Tract.

TOTAL AREA :

    The total area of Kashmir is approximately 222,236 to 222,738 square kilometers (85,806 to 86,000 square miles), depending on the source. Some estimates suggest it could be around 225,000 square kilometers (87,000 square miles). The region is known for its stunning mountainous terrain and is divided between India, Pakistan, and China.

KASHMIR UNDER PAKISTAN :

    The total area of Kashmir under Pakistan is approximately 30%, which includes Azad Kashmir and Gilgit-Baltistan.

KASHMIR UNDER INDIA :

    The area of Kashmir under India is approximately 55%, which includes the Indian-administered territories of Jammu and Kashmir and Ladakh. The total area of Kashmir is about 222,200 square kilometers, with India controlling around 106,567 square kilometers of this region. The remaining areas are controlled by Pakistan and China, with Pakistan controlling about 30% and China holding the remaining 15%.

KASHMIR UNDER CHINA :

    The area of Kashmir under China primarily refers to Aksai Chin, which is a region administered by China. Aksai Chin covers approximately 38,000 square kilometers (15,000 square miles) and is characterized by its high-altitude desert terrain, bordered by the Karakoram Range and Kunlun Mountains. This region is part of the larger Kashmir region that has been a subject of dispute between India and China since 1959, with India claiming it as part of Ladakh and China administering it as part of its Xinjiang and Tibet autonomous regions.
