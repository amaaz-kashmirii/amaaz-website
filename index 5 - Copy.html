Multi-Party System and Coalition Era in the Politics of Jammu and Kashmir :

The political landscape of Jammu and Kashmir has seen a significant shift since 2002, transitioning from a dominant single-party rule to a multi-party system characterized by coalition governments. This change has brought new challenges and opportunities, shaping the state's political trajectory. The multi-party system and coalition era have brought increased political representation and participation, but they also face significant challenges, including political instability and inefficiency. To move forward, it is essential to address these challenges and promote more inclusive and accountable decision-making. This requires strong leadership, political will, and a commitment to democratic values.

GOVERNMENT STRUCTURE

The government structure of Azad Kashmir consists of a President as the head of state and a Prime Minister as the chief executive, supported by a council of ministers. The Azad Kashmir Legislative Assembly serves as the unicameral legislature, consisting of 41 elected members and eight co-opted members, including women and technocrats. The government operates under the AJK Interim Constitution Act of 1974, which establishes its own executive and legislative frameworks, differing from those of Pakistan's provinces.

HIGH COURTS OF AJK

MIRPUR HIGH COURT:

The High Court of Azad Jammu and Kashmir, Mirpur Bench, is a significant institution in the region, serving as an appellate court for the District Courts. The court's mission is to provide a fair and impartial forum for the resolution of disputes, ensuring that court decisions are based on the law and facts of a given case, not on political convictions, personal views, or religious beliefs. The court's website offers timely and relevant information to improve access to justice and maintain confidence in the judiciary.

RAWLAKOT HIGH COURT:

The High Court of Azad Jammu and Kashmir, with its circuit benches in Kotli, Mirpur, and Rawalakot, plays a crucial role in the judicial system of the region. It hears appeals from the District Courts and has been instrumental in upholding the constitution and the rule of law. The court's circuit benches in Rawalakot provide broader regional access, allowing citizens and organizations to challenge decisions or actions of the executive authorities. The High Court's jurisdiction includes constitutional petitions, civil and criminal appeals, and revisional and supervisory jurisdiction over lower courts and tribunals.

KOTLI HIGH COURT:

The High Court of Azad Jammu and Kashmir, commonly known as the Azad Kashmir High Court, is an appellate court located in Muzaffarabad, Azad Jammu and Kashmir. It hears appeals from the District Courts within the region and operates with three circuit benches situated in Kotli, Mirpur, and Rawalakot. The court's primary mission is to provide a fair and impartial forum for the resolution of disputes, ensuring that court decisions are based on the law and facts of a given case, not on political convictions, personal views, or religious beliefs of those involved. The High Court of Azad Jammu and Kashmir was established on May 18, 1948, making it a long-standing judicial institution in the region. It serves as the primary appellate body for the entire Azad Jammu and Kashmir region, with the principal seat of the High Court located in Muzaffarabad. The court's jurisdiction extends to all areas within the region, and it operates under Section 43 of the Azad Jammu and Kashmir Interim Constitution Act 1974, which defines its powers, structure, and functions. The High Court of Azad Jammu and Kashmir has been instrumental in maintaining the confidence in the judiciary and improving access to justice in the region. It has been involved in various legal matters, including the appointment of Chief Election Commissioners and the resolution of disputes involving the Azad Government. SUPREME COURT OF AJK

The Supreme Court of Azad Jammu and Kashmir is the highest appellate court in the region, consisting of a Chief Justice and two other judges, as per the AJK Interim Constitution Act, 1974. The Chief Justice is currently Raja Saeed Akram Khan, who was appointed on May 18, 2021. The court is responsible for resolving constitutional, civil, and criminal cases, and it serves as a guardian of the AJK Interim Constitution, ensuring the fundamental rights of citizens are upheld.
